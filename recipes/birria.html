Birria Recipe

Birria is a traditional Mexican dish renowned for its rich flavors and tender meat. Originating from the state of Jalisco, it has gained popularity across the globe for its comforting and hearty qualities. At its core, birria is a stew featuring succulent, slow-cooked meat, typically goat or beef, infused with a blend of aromatic spices and chilies. The dish boasts a deep red color and a tantalizing aroma that fills the air as it simmers.

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs beef or goat meat (bone-in for richer flavor)
  • 5 dried guajillo chilies
  • 3 dried ancho chilies
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas, lime wedges, chopped onions, and fresh cilantro for serving

Steps:

  1. Remove stems and seeds from dried chilies. Toast them lightly in a dry skillet, then soak them in hot water for about 15 minutes.
  2. In a blender, combine soaked chilies, chopped onion, minced garlic, oregano, cumin, cloves, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth, adding water if needed to achieve a pourable consistency.
  3. Cut the meat into large chunks and place them in a large pot. Pour the chili mixture over the meat, ensuring it's fully coated. Marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.
  4. Add enough water to the pot to cover the meat. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer, covered, for 2-3 hours or until the meat is tender and falls apart easily.
  5. Once the meat is cooked, remove it from the pot and shred it using two forks. Skim any excess fat from the broth.
  6. Serve the shredded meat with warm corn tortillas, lime wedges, chopped onions, fresh cilantro, and a bowl of the flavorful broth for dipping or as a soup. Enjoy the rich, aromatic flavors of this classic Mexican dish!
